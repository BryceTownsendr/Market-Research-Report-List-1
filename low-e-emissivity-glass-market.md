<p><p>Low-E (emissivity) glass is a type of energy-efficient glass that is coated with a thin metallic layer to reduce heat transfer. It is commonly used in windows, doors, and skylights for both residential and commercial buildings.</p><p>The future of the Low-E glass market looks promising due to the increasing focus on energy conservation and sustainability. With rising concerns about climate change and energy efficiency, there is a growing demand for energy-saving solutions in the construction industry. As a result, the market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years.</p><p>In addition to the residential and commercial construction sectors, the automotive industry is also adopting Low-E glass to enhance the energy efficiency of vehicles.
