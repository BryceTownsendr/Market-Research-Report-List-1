<p><p>Railway propulsion systems are used to power trains and locomotives. There are two main types: diesel propulsion systems and electric locomotive propulsion systems.</p><p>Diesel propulsion systems use diesel engines to generate mechanical energy, which is then transformed into kinetic energy, propelling the train forward. These systems are commonly used in areas without electrified railway lines or where flexibility is required.</p><p>Electric locomotive propulsion systems, on the other hand, rely on electric power to drive the train. They use overhead power lines or third rail systems to supply electricity, which is then converted into mechanical energy, enabling the train to move. These systems are more environmentally friendly and efficient, especially in areas with electrified routes.</p></p>
