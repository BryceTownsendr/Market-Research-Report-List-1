<p><p>V2X, or Vehicle-to-Everything, refers to the communication technology used in the automotive sector to enable vehicles to connect with various entities. V2V, or Vehicle-to-Vehicle, allows vehicles to directly communicate with one another, sharing information about their position, speed, and intentions, enhancing safety on the road. V2I, or Vehicle-to-Infrastructure, enables vehicles to communicate with roadside infrastructure, such as traffic lights, for improved traffic management and congestion reduction. V2P, or Vehicle-to-Pedestrian, focuses on allowing vehicles to interact with pedestrians to prevent accidents. Other market types in V2X include V2N (Vehicle-to-Network) and V2D (Vehicle-to-Device), facilitating communication with network and personal devices for advanced functionalities.</p></p>
